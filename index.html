Mini Pupper Laser Tag Project

Welcome to the official page of the Mini Pupper Laser Tag Project! Our team, consisting of Nicholas Barden, Jace Palmer, Kevin Sabbe, Kamen Schaefle, and Jude Williams, created a robotic system for playing laser tag using the Mini Pupper robot. Below, you'll find detailed descriptions of our project, including our goals, approach, and accomplishments.

Project Description

Our project involved creating a laser tag game with the Mini Pupper where it can locate a target and "tag" it using an IR laser/receiver system. The robot can detect AR tags to locate its target and distinguish between different types of hits.

Goals and Approach

Goals:

  • Locate and "tag" a stationary target with an AR tag.
  • Locate and "tag" a human target with an AR tag on their leg.
  • Locate and "tag" another Mini Pupper while avoiding being tagged itself.

Approach:

We developed IR communication and visual laser detection, enabling the robot to aim and fire at AR tag targets. The robot also included capabilities for walking, turning, and other movement commands.

Successes

Working Features:

  • IR and Visual Laser Detection: Differentiates between regular and critical hits.
  • Searching and Aiming: Robot can locate and aim at AR tags.
  • Tag Detection: The Mini Pupper shows a "hit" state when tagged.
  • AR Tag Detection: Uses webcam to identify AR tags.
  • Movement: Commands for walking, turning, and looking around.

Challenges

Ongoing Challenges:

  • Accurate Targeting: Stabilizing the laser and improving rotation precision.
  • Moving Target: Enhancing movement tracking for dynamic targets.
  • Self-tagging: Preventing the robot from tagging itself with the IR laser.

Team Members' Reflections

  • Jace: Learned the complexity of kinematics calculations.
  • Jude: Gained insight into environmental navigation.
  • Kamen: Improved Python and AR tag detection skills with OpenCV.
  • Kevin: Enhanced understanding of Arduino, Raspberry Pi communication, and practical coding applications.
  • Nic: Learned more about Python and interacting with social robots.
